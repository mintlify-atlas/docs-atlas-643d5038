The research hypothesis establishes the expected impact of City Report as an integrated system for municipal-citizen communication.

## Central hypothesis

<Info>
  The implementation of **City Report** as an integrated citizen report management system facilitates and strengthens communication between the municipality and citizens, by establishing a structured, transparent, and efficient digital channel that improves the registration, tracking, and attention to urban problems, contributing to a closer, more reliable, and organized relationship between both parties.
</Info>

## Hypothesis scope

This hypothesis addresses City Report as a unified solution, without segmenting by platforms, and focuses on its communicational, social, and operational impact within the municipal context.

## Research approach

The hypothesis is designed to evaluate City Report holistically:

- **Unified system perspective**: Considers both web and mobile platforms as parts of an integrated solution
- **Communication-centric**: Emphasizes the strengthening of municipal-citizen communication channels
- **Transparency focus**: Evaluates how the system promotes institutional transparency
- **Efficiency measurement**: Assesses improvements in urban problem management processes
- **Relationship building**: Analyzes the system's role in fostering closer municipal-citizen relations

<Note>
  The hypothesis positions City Report not merely as a technological tool, but as a facilitator of citizen participation and the bond between government and society.
</Note>
