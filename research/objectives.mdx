The City Report research aims to evaluate how the web and mobile platforms optimize urban report management and strengthen municipal-citizen communication.

## General objective

Evaluate the functional and operational impact of **City Report (web and mobile)** as a technological solution for the efficient management of urban reports in the municipal sphere.

<Note>
  This research focuses on both web and mobile versions as complementary components of a unified digital citizen service model.
</Note>

## Specific objectives

The research investigates four key areas:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Web platform organization">
    Analyze how the web version improves the organization, classification, and internal control of citizen reports.
    
    The web platform serves as the operational hub for authorized municipal personnel to centralize, manage, and track urban reports.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mobile platform citizen experience">
    Identify the contribution of the mobile version to the citizen experience by enabling clear, fast, and accessible queries.
    
    The mobile app empowers citizens to consult and track their reports, promoting transparency, institutional trust, and social participation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Platform integration">
    Compare the role of both platforms within the citizen service flow, highlighting their functional integration.
    
    Both versions strategically complement each other to consolidate a modern, accessible, and controlled digital citizen service model.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Decision-making impact">
    Determine if the system, as a whole, contributes to better municipal decision-making and efficient use of public resources.
    
    The research evaluates how City Report enables data-driven decisions and resource optimization.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## User demographics

The research considers two distinct user segments:

### Citizens

<Card title="Age range: 18-65 years" icon="users">
  This segment includes adults with basic digital interaction skills and ability to use technological devices.
  
  **Justification:** This range encompasses the legally adult population with the highest likelihood of actively participating in citizen reports and possessing sufficient digital skills to consult information, understand status updates, and track urban incidents.
</Card>

### Municipal personnel

<Card title="Age range: 22-60 years" icon="briefcase">
  This segment consists of administrative and operational workers with responsibilities in the report management process.
  
  **Justification:** This range reflects common employment profiles within public institutions, considering both young personnel in administrative and technological roles, as well as more experienced personnel in supervisory and decision-making positions.
</Card>
