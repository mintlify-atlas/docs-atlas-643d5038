City Report serves two distinct user segments, each with specific needs and capabilities within the municipal reporting ecosystem.

## Citizens

The citizen segment consists of individuals with basic digital interaction skills and the ability to use technological devices. The target age range is **18 to 65 years**.

### Characteristics

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Age range" icon="users">
    18 to 65 years old
  </Card>
  <Card title="Digital capability" icon="mobile">
    Basic digital literacy and device usage
  </Card>
  <Card title="Legal status" icon="id-card">
    Legally adult population
  </Card>
  <Card title="Participation level" icon="chart-line">
    Active in urban reporting and civic engagement
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Why this age range?

This demographic is selected because it encompasses:

- The legally adult population with the capacity to participate in civic reporting
- Individuals most likely to actively engage with urban issues
- Users with sufficient digital skills to consult information, understand status updates, and track urban incidents
- A broad cross-section of the community that regularly interacts with municipal services

<Info>
  The mobile version of City Report is specifically designed to meet citizen needs, providing clear, fast, and accessible ways to consult and track reports while promoting transparency and institutional trust.
</Info>

## Municipal staff

The municipal staff segment comprises administrative and operational workers with responsibilities in the report management process. The estimated age range is **22 to 60 years**.

### Characteristics

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Age range" icon="briefcase">
    22 to 60 years old
  </Card>
  <Card title="Role types" icon="sitemap">
    Administrative and operational positions
  </Card>
  <Card title="Experience levels" icon="stairs">
    From entry-level tech roles to senior decision-makers
  </Card>
  <Card title="Responsibilities" icon="tasks">
    Report processing, tracking, and resolution
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Why this age range?

This demographic reflects typical employment profiles within public institutions, including:

- Younger staff (22-35) in administrative and technological functions
- Mid-career professionals (35-50) managing operational workflows
- Experienced personnel (50-60) in supervisory roles and decision-making positions
- A range that captures diverse skill sets from digital-native workers to experienced public administrators

<Info>
  The web version of City Report is designed for municipal staff, centralizing report administration and enabling operational tracking by authorized personnel.
</Info>

## Complementary design

Both user segments work together within the City Report ecosystem:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Citizens submit reports">
    Using the mobile app, citizens document and submit urban issues from their smartphones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Municipal staff processes reports">
    Through the web platform, authorized personnel organize, classify, and manage incoming reports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Citizens track progress">
    The mobile app allows citizens to monitor the status of their reports in real-time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff coordinates resolution">
    Municipal teams use the web interface to coordinate responses and update report statuses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The strategic complementarity between these two segments and their respective platforms creates a modern, accessible, and controlled digital model for citizen services.
</Note>
