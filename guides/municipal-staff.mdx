City Report's web platform provides municipal staff with centralized tools to manage urban issue reports, coordinate responses, and maintain transparent communication with citizens.

## Getting started

The web application is designed for municipal workers aged 22-60 in administrative and operational roles. You'll use it to receive, classify, assign, track, and resolve citizen reports efficiently.

### Your responsibilities

As municipal staff, you play a crucial role in:

- Reviewing incoming citizen reports
- Classifying issues by type and priority
- Assigning work to appropriate departments or teams
- Updating report statuses as work progresses
- Maintaining accurate records for institutional accountability
- Supporting data-driven decision-making through proper documentation

## Managing incoming reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the dashboard">
    Log in to the City Report web platform to view your personalized dashboard showing pending reports, active cases, and alerts.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review new reports">
    Navigate to the "New Reports" queue to see submissions that need initial review.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Examine report details">
    Click on each report to view:
    - Citizen description of the issue
    - Photos and attached evidence
    - GPS location and address
    - Submission date and time
    - Reporter contact information (if provided)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify information">
    Check that the report contains sufficient detail and falls within municipal jurisdiction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Process reports promptly to maintain citizen trust. Even if immediate action isn't possible, updating the status shows that the municipality is responsive.
</Tip>

## Classifying reports

Proper classification ensures that reports are routed to the right departments and prioritized appropriately.

### Classification categories

**Issue type**: Categorize by the nature of the problem (infrastructure, public services, maintenance, safety, etc.)

**Severity level**: Assess urgency based on:
- Public safety impact
- Number of people affected
- Potential for escalation
- Legal or regulatory requirements

**Department responsibility**: Determine which team or department should handle the issue.

**Estimated timeline**: Set realistic expectations for resolution based on issue complexity and available resources.

### Classification workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Analyze the issue">
    Review all provided information, including photos and location data, to understand the problem fully.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign categories">
    Select the appropriate issue type, priority level, and responsible department from the system's classification options.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set priority">
    Mark urgent issues (safety hazards, service outages) with high priority for immediate attention.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add internal notes">
    Include any observations or context that will help the assigned team address the issue effectively.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update status">
    Change the report status from "Received" to "Under Review" to indicate active processing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Misclassification can lead to delays, resource waste, or escalated problems. Take time to classify reports accurately rather than rushing through the queue.
</Warning>

## Operational workflows

### Assigning work

Once reports are classified, assign them to the appropriate teams:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the report">
    From your dashboard, choose a classified report ready for assignment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose the team">
    Assign the report to the department, crew, or individual responsible for that type of work.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set expectations">
    Include any special instructions, deadlines, or resource requirements.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Notify the team">
    The system automatically alerts assigned personnel about their new work order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Tracking progress

Maintain oversight of all active reports:

**Monitor status changes**: Track reports as they move through workflow stages (received, under review, in progress, resolved).

**Check response times**: Identify bottlenecks or delays that need management attention.

**Review team workload**: Balance assignments to prevent overload and ensure efficient resource use.

**Follow up on stalled cases**: Investigate reports that haven't progressed within expected timeframes.

<Note>
  The web platform provides filtering and sorting tools to help you manage large volumes of reports. Use these features to organize your work efficiently.
</Note>

### Updating citizens

Maintain transparent communication by updating report statuses:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the report">
    Open the report that needs a status update.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Change the status">
    Select the new status that reflects current progress:
    - **In progress**: Work has begun
    - **Resolved**: Issue has been addressed
    - **Closed**: Report is complete and archived
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add resolution notes">
    When resolving a report, document what actions were taken, materials used, or reasons for closure.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and notify">
    Save your changes. Citizens automatically receive notifications about status updates through the mobile app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Detailed resolution notes create valuable institutional knowledge and help with similar issues in the future. Document your work thoroughly.
</Tip>

## Improving efficiency

### Standardizing processes

**Use templates**: Create standard responses for common issue types to speed up processing.

**Establish protocols**: Follow consistent classification criteria to ensure fair and predictable handling.

**Coordinate with teams**: Maintain regular communication with field crews and other departments to streamline workflows.

**Track metrics**: Monitor response times, resolution rates, and citizen satisfaction to identify improvement opportunities.

### Leveraging data

City Report centralizes information that supports better decision-making:

**Identify patterns**: Analyze report data to spot recurring issues in specific areas or infrastructure systems.

**Allocate resources**: Use historical data to predict demand and position crews or materials strategically.

**Plan maintenance**: Shift from reactive to proactive maintenance by identifying problems before they escalate.

**Justify budgets**: Support budget requests with concrete data about issue frequency and resolution costs.

<Note>
  The system's reporting and analytics features help you demonstrate the value of your work and advocate for resources your department needs.
</Note>

## Best practices

### Quality assurance

**Verify completeness**: Ensure all reports have sufficient information before assignment. Request clarification from citizens if needed.

**Document thoroughly**: Accurate records protect the municipality legally and provide continuity when staff changes occur.

**Follow up on resolutions**: Periodically review closed reports to ensure issues were genuinely resolved and haven't recurred.

**Maintain consistency**: Apply the same standards to all reports regardless of source or location.

### Communication excellence

**Be responsive**: Update report statuses promptly, even if it's just to acknowledge receipt and set expectations.

**Be clear**: Write resolution notes that citizens can understand, avoiding overly technical language.

**Be professional**: Maintain a helpful, courteous tone in all communications, remembering that you represent the municipality.

**Be transparent**: When issues can't be resolved immediately, explain why and provide realistic timelines.

<Warning>
  Citizen reports contain personal information. Follow your municipality's data privacy policies and never share report details outside authorized channels.
</Warning>

## System administration

For staff with administrative responsibilities:

### User management

- Create and manage staff accounts with appropriate permission levels
- Assign users to departments or teams for proper routing
- Monitor user activity to ensure system security and proper usage
- Deactivate accounts when staff members leave or change roles

### Configuration

- Customize classification categories to match your municipality's structure
- Set up automated routing rules based on issue types or locations
- Configure notification settings for different user roles
- Establish service level agreements (SLAs) for response times

### Reporting

- Generate reports on system usage, resolution times, and issue trends
- Create executive summaries for municipal leadership
- Export data for integration with other municipal systems
- Archive historical records according to retention policies

<Tip>
  Regular system maintenance and configuration updates keep City Report aligned with your municipality's evolving needs and organizational structure.
</Tip>
