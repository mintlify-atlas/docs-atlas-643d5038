City Report's mobile platform enables you to report urban issues, track their progress, and engage with your municipal government through a transparent digital channel.

## Getting started

The mobile application is designed for citizens aged 18-65 with basic digital literacy. You can use it to report issues like damaged infrastructure, public service problems, or urban maintenance needs.

### What you can do

- Submit detailed reports about urban issues in your area
- Attach photos and location data to your reports
- Track the status of your submissions in real-time
- View response times and municipal actions
- Access transparent information about report handling

## Submitting a report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the mobile app">
    Launch City Report on your mobile device and log in to your account.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create a new report">
    Tap the "New Report" button and select the type of issue you want to report.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add details">
    Provide a clear description of the issue, including:
    - What the problem is
    - Where it's located
    - When you noticed it
    - How it affects you or your community
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Attach evidence">
    Take photos of the issue to help municipal staff understand the situation. Images provide crucial context for proper classification and response.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm location">
    Verify the GPS location or manually adjust the pin on the map to ensure accuracy.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit">
    Review your report and submit it. You'll receive a confirmation with a tracking number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Include as much detail as possible in your report. Clear descriptions and quality photos help municipal staff classify and prioritize your issue correctly.
</Tip>

## Tracking your reports

Once you submit a report, you can monitor its progress through several status stages:

### Understanding report statuses

**Received**: Your report has been successfully submitted and logged in the system.

**Under review**: Municipal staff are evaluating your report and determining the appropriate response.

**In progress**: Work has begun to address the issue you reported.

**Resolved**: The issue has been addressed. You'll see details about the actions taken.

**Closed**: The report has been completed and archived.

### Checking status updates

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access your reports">
    Open the app and navigate to "My Reports" to see all your submissions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a report">
    Tap on any report to view its current status and history.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review updates">
    See timestamps, status changes, and any notes from municipal staff about actions taken.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Response times vary depending on the severity and type of issue. Emergency situations are prioritized over routine maintenance requests.
</Note>

## Understanding transparency

City Report promotes transparency by giving you visibility into how your municipal government handles citizen reports.

### What transparency means for you

**Clear communication**: You receive updates at each stage of the process, eliminating uncertainty about whether your report was received or is being addressed.

**Traceability**: Every report has a complete history showing when it was submitted, reviewed, assigned, and resolved.

**Accountability**: Municipal staff actions are documented, creating a record of institutional response to community needs.

**Data-driven decisions**: Your reports contribute to a broader dataset that helps the municipality identify patterns, allocate resources, and improve urban services.

### Building trust

The system strengthens the relationship between you and your municipal government by:

- Establishing a direct digital channel for communication
- Reducing information loss through structured data collection
- Demonstrating institutional commitment to addressing urban issues
- Empowering you to participate actively in improving your community

<Tip>
  Regular use of the platform and detailed reporting help build a comprehensive picture of urban needs, leading to better municipal planning and resource allocation.
</Tip>

## Best practices

### Writing effective reports

**Be specific**: Describe exactly what's wrong and where it's located. Vague descriptions delay response times.

**Use photos**: Visual evidence helps staff assess the situation without needing to visit the location first.

**Check for duplicates**: Before submitting, search existing reports to see if someone else has already reported the same issue.

**Provide context**: Explain how the issue affects daily life or safety. This helps with prioritization.

### Managing your account

**Keep notifications enabled**: You'll receive alerts when your report status changes.

**Update your profile**: Ensure your contact information is current in case municipal staff need to reach you.

**Review resolved reports**: Check completed work to verify that issues were properly addressed.

<Warning>
  Only report genuine issues that require municipal attention. False or frivolous reports waste public resources and may affect your account standing.
</Warning>
